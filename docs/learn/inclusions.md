# Inclusions

Diagnostic inclusions and fingerprinting techniques for gem identification.

!!! tip "Interactive Version"
    For detailed data tables and interactive content, visit [gemmology.dev/learn/inclusions](https://gemmology.dev/learn/inclusions).

## Types of Inclusions

### Solid Inclusions

Crystals of other minerals trapped during growth.

| Host Gem | Common Inclusions |
|----------|-------------------|
| Ruby/Sapphire | Rutile silk, zircon halos, calcite |
| Emerald | Pyrite, calcite, tremolite, mica |
| Diamond | Garnet, olivine, chromite |

### Fluid Inclusions

- **Two-phase** - Liquid + gas bubble
- **Three-phase** - Liquid + gas + solid crystal (diagnostic for Colombian emerald)
- **Fingerprint inclusions** - Healed fractures with fluid remnants

### Growth Features

- Colour zoning
- Growth tubes
- Twinning
- Phantoms

## Origin Determination

### Corundum by Origin

| Origin | Characteristic Inclusions |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Burma (Ruby) | Short rutile silk, calcite, swirl patterns |
| Kashmir (Sapphire) | Tourmaline, zircon halos, milky zones |
| Sri Lanka | Long rutile silk, hexagonal colour zoning |

### Emerald by Origin

| Origin | Characteristic Inclusions |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Colombia | Three-phase inclusions with halite cube |
| Zambia | Black mica flakes, amphibole needles |
| Brazil | Biotite mica, pyrite |

## Natural vs Synthetic

| Natural Indicators | Synthetic Indicators |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| Mineral inclusions | Curved striae |
| Three-phase inclusions | Gas bubbles |
| Fingerprint patterns | Flux inclusions |
| Irregular zoning | Chevron zoning |
